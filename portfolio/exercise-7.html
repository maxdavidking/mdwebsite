Exercise 7: Texture

The stone texture is pockmarked and striated by scratches and holes in the cement. If it weren't so smooth in all other places it could be seen as the surface of the moon, as the colour ranges from light to dark gray as well. The pockmarks are also unevenly clustered in certain areas, whereas other areas of the surface are quite smooth. It's pattern is clearly man made and synthetic. The earth texture is uneven and pocked as well, this time by different pebbles and coloured dots of white and yellow from the plant fertilizer used in the earth. Darker browns from larger twigs and mulch are found in the image as well. It's a very similar picture, yet the utter lack of pattern and straight lines marks it differently and from the stone, and the colours (brown, yellow, white) are much softer than the harsh gray of the concrete.
