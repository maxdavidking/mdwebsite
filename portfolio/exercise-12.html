Exercise 12: Modular Grid

I think, without a doubt, that the complex design is more successful. One of the points that I made during my last assignment's write up was that I aspire to a sense of minimalism in design. That still remains true, but minimalism still needs to use a wide variety of design methods to communicate use. Without the tools of hue, typography and scale you can only get so far. Using grid and layout effectively is important and can achieve many goals, but using all (or most) of these tools in coordination is hugely important. That might seem to contradict my argument for minimalism, but I would argue that minimalism isn't the absence of design styles, it's the effective and subtle use of them. So, for example, adding italics to my figure caption, colour to my headings and menu items, and a stronger, more legible font to my text are all design choices that are quite minimal, but hopefully effective to further communicate the purpose of each block of content.
