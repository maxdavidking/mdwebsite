Exercise 6: Scale

What I found with this exercise was that having a flat background really helped to make the scale more ambiguous. The moment the photo has a clear marker (a window, a door, a human being) to indicate what the "correct" scale should be it loses it's illusion. The other thing I found interesting was to place different items in the foreground of the image to mess with their sense of scale. The board game jewel I have in the foreground is by far the smallest item, but takes up much of the photos real estate and consequently looks larger.
